What to do if your child has a chipped tooth?

  • Home
  • Blog
  • What to do if your child has a chipped tooth?

Your kid comes running into your home with seemingly a bloody lip. You will quickly come to know that he or she has a chip missing out from their tooth.

What do you do when this occurs? Many parents will experience this from time-time. Accidents will happen, yet chipped teeth, particularly those in a kid, can be extremely difficult and need proper care in reducing the pain and minimizing the risk of infection.

If your kid does chip a tooth, it's essential to have him examined by a child's emergency dentist as quickly as possible, regardless of whether the damage shows up to be minor. Indeed, even a small chip or crack could have long-lasting consequences for your child's oral well-being. There could be an opening that is not visible to the naked eye yet permits oral bacteria to enter the tooth's sensitive inner core and lead to infection.

Get Chipped tooth treatment for your child from a Dental surgeon in Narasaraopet

There are some steps that parents ought to take at home before getting to the dental specialist, as well.

To start with, wash out your child's mouth. If any swelling is present, you can lessen it with an ice pack or cold pack. If possible, safeguard the tooth fragment and carry it with you to the dental appointment in case it very well may be re-attached.

The dentist will completely examine your kid and either does the reattachment with the tooth fragment, if it is available and that treatment is possible, or re-contour the tooth to eliminate any rugged edges or may utilize a bonding material in filling the space in cases of large chips.

The dentist will likewise give you instructions for really caring for the tooth at home after it is treated. Your kid might have to make adjustments to their day-to-day routine for only a couple of days after the tooth is repaired, contingent upon the intervention utilized.

The key to managing any type of dental emergency, including a chipped tooth, is to have a plan ahead of time with the goal you can understand what to do if and when the situation emerges.
