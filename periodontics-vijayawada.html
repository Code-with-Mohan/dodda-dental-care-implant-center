Periodontics is a specialized branch of dentistry focused on the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of periodontal (gum) diseases, as well as the placement and maintenance of dental implants. Periodontists are trained to handle complex gum-related issues, including gingivitis and periodontitis, which can lead to tooth loss if untreated. Periodontist in Narasaraopet assess the health of the gums, the supporting structures of the teeth, and the bone that holds them in place. Treatment options provided by periodontists include non-surgical therapies like scaling and root planing, surgical interventions such as flap surgery and bone grafts, and the application of regenerative techniques to restore damaged tissues. Additionally, periodontists are experts in managing conditions like gum recession and can perform cosmetic procedures to enhance the aesthetics of the gum line, improving both function and appearance. Regular periodontal evaluations are crucial for maintaining oral health, especially for individuals with risk factors like diabetes or a history of gum disease.

Symptoms of Gum diseases

  1. Red or Swollen Gums: Healthy gums are pink; redness and swelling are often signs of inflammation
  2. Bleeding Gums: Whether during brushing, flossing, or spontaneously, bleeding gums indicate potential gum disease.
  3. Bad Breath: Persistent halitosis can result from bacteria buildup and gum infections.
  4. Receding Gums: Gums pulling away from teeth can expose tooth roots, often signaling periodontal issues.
  5. Loose Teeth: Advanced gum disease can affect tooth stability, leading to looseness over time.
  6. Changes in Bite: A transforming bite or shifting teeth can indicate gum or bone loss.
  7. Tooth Sensitivity: Increased sensitivity, especially to hot or cold, may arise due to gum recession exposing tooth roots.
  8. Pus Between Gums and Teeth: Pus or discharge is a clear sign of infection and should be addressed immediately.
  9. Persistent Pain: Ongoing discomfort or pain in the gums can signal serious periodontal issues requiring professional intervention.

Looking into phases of gum diseases:

Gingivitis:

The initial stage of gum disease, marked by inflammation and redness of the gums (gingiva). This occurs due to poor oral hygiene, bacterial plaque accumulation, and tartar buildup. Symptoms include red, swollen, and bleeding gums, and can be reversible with good oral care habits and professional cleaning.

Treatment Options:
  1. Professional Cleaning (Scaling and Polishing): Removes plaque and tartar from the teeth, helping to stop the progression of the disease.
  2. Improved Oral Hygiene: Encourages patients to brush and floss regularly to prevent the buildup of plaque.
  3. Fluoride Treatment: Provides additional protection for the teeth and helps prevent further decay.

Mild Periodontitis:

The next stage, where the inflammation has progressed to the supporting structures of the teeth, including the periodontal ligament and alveolar bone. This stage may present with minimal attachment loss and slight bone loss needing gum contouring.

Treatment Options:
  1. Antibiotics: May be prescribed to help control the bacterial infection and fight off the disease.
  2. Scaling and Root Planing (Deep Cleaning): A more invasive version of professional cleaning, which removes plaque and tartar from below the gum line.
  3. Antibiotic Gel or Ointment: Applied directly to the infected area to combat the bacterial infection.
  4. Laser Treatment: Uses a laser to remove damaged tissue and bacteria, promoting healing and reducing the need for surgery at gum surgery clinic in Narasaraopet.

Moderate Periodontitis:

In this stage, significant damage to the periodontal ligament and alveolar bone has occurred, resulting in moderate attachment loss and noticeable bone loss. The gums recede, exposing more of the tooth and roots.

Advanced Periodontitis (Periodontosis):

The most severe stage of gum infections, characterized by deep pocket development, extensive bone loss, and potential tooth mobility. At this stage, teeth are at risk of falling out, and advanced treatments like tooth extraction, bone regeneration, or dental implants might be necessary to stabilize the remaining teeth and restore the patient's oral function. If left untreated, gum disease can progress to periodontosis, which can lead to tooth loss and other systemic complications.   2. Bone Grafting: To restore lost bone due to gum disease, allowing for more stability and a healthier smile.
  3. Implant Restoration: To replace missing teeth, restoring chewing function and overall oral health.
  4. Gum Transplant: To cover and stabilize the exposed roots of a tooth.
