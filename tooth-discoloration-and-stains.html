What can cause tooth discoloration and stains?

  • Home
  • Blog
  • What can cause tooth discoloration and stains?

Have you seen your teeth aren't as white as they used to be and perhaps not quite as white as you'd like?

Here's what you need to be aware of the reasons for tooth discoloration and stains, and how you might keep your pearly whites looking their best.

Extrinsic discoloration

This happens when the external layer of the enamel turns out to be stained because of the consumption of specific substances. Consumables that can cause tooth discoloration to include:

  1. Food and drink like coffee, tea, wine, cola, tobacco, and certain fruits and vegetables like apples and potatoes
  2. Smoking or chewing tobacco
  3. Poor dental hygiene results in the accumulation of stain-causing particles

Intrinsic discoloration

This happens when the interior structure of the tooth, the dentin, obscures or fosters a yellow shade. This can be caused by various things:

  1. Exposure to an excessive amount of fluoride during childhood
  2. Chipped teeth can make discoloration due to nerve or enamel damage
  3. Meds like tetracycline and doxycycline antibiotics, a few allergy medicines, antipsychotics, and antihypertensives
  4. Several diseases can influence the color of the enamel or dentin. Furthermore, treatment, like chemotherapy can likewise cause staining

How can you prevent tooth discoloration?

Having a good dental hygiene routine can help in preventing extrinsic tooth discoloration. This incorporates brushing your teeth after each meal. the best dental specialist for teeth cleaning in Narasaraopet likewise suggests rinsing your mouth with water after consuming food varieties or beverages that could stain your teeth, like tea, coffee, wine, or cola. Visiting a dental hygienist every 6 months for a professional cleaning will likewise aid in the elimination of extrinsic stains.

When to see a dentist?

If you have noticed a change in your teeth color and it gets worse, it's a good idea to see a the Best dentist in Narasaraopet.

If the staining seems to be more deeper, and if no over-the-counter whitening agents can get rid of the staining, it could be something more serious, like a cavity or demineralization of enamel.

If you have one tooth stained or discolored, it could be because of a cavity or an injury inside your tooth. The sooner these kinds of issues get treated by your dentist, the better the result will probably be.
