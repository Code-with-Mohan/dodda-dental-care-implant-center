  • Can brushing your tongue damage your taste buds?

Tongue scraping is a process where you drag a tool from the rear of your tongue to the front to eliminate small particles and bacteria. There are various sizes and varieties of tongue scrapers. Some are U-shaped devices, and others are small loops with a handle on the end.

Tongue scrapers can benefit your health and assist you with getting that immaculate feel, however, they can damage your taste buds if you do it excessively hard.

What happens if you utilize a tongue scrapper too hard?

If you press too hard, the tongue scrapper can damage your taste buds. If you utilize the tool accurately with light pressure, however, it shouldn't do any harm or cause any issues.

Ensure your scraper has no sharp edges that could lead to a physical injury. If your tongue bleeds, quit utilizing the scraper.

How to utilize a tongue scrubber

Tongue scratching is a basic interaction. To begin, you'll have to track down the right instrument. You can often find an inexpensive plastic one at your local drugstore. You can find copper and stainless steel versions, as well.

Follow these tongue-scraping steps:

  1. Brush your teeth with fluoride toothpaste for 2 minutes.
  2. Follow up with floss and wash like you normally would.
  3. Facing a mirror, stick out your tongue and delicately put the tongue scraper on the back of your tongue.
  4. Pull the scraper forward with light pressure.
  5. Rinse the tongue scraper in warm water.
  6. Place it back on your tongue and repeat until you have scraped your entire tongue. One to two scrapes for each part of your tongue is sufficient.
  7. Wash the tongue scraper with soap and water, then rinse and dry it. Store it somewhere clean and dry, similar to a case or upstanding in a toothbrush holder.
  8. Rinse your mouth with water.
