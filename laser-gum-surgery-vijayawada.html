Laser Gum Surgery:

Laser Gum Surgery in Narasaraopet, also known as laser periodontal therapy, is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat gum disease and improve gum health using focused laser technology. The procedure targets infected or damaged gum tissue, removing bacteria, diseased tissue, and plaque without the need for traditional scalpel and sutures. It begins with the dentist numbing the area with local anesthesia, then using a specialized laser device to precisely cut or vaporize the diseased tissue. The process often involves scaling and root planing, where plaque and tartar beneath the gum line are cleaned away, followed by smoothing the roots to encourage reattachment of healthy tissue. Laser gum surgery offers advantages such as reduced pain, swelling, and recovery time, as well as less trauma to surrounding tissues. It is suitable for treating various stages of gum disease, from mild gingivitis to more advanced periodontitis.

Different procedures used under laser dentistry:

  1. Cavity Removal (Veneering and Restorations)
  2. Gum Disease Treatment (Gingivectomy): Lasers effectively remove infected gum tissue, promoting faster healing and blood clot formation, which reduces bleeding and post-operative pain.
  3. Teeth Whitening: Laser-activated bleaching agents accelerate the whitening process, providing quick and effective cosmetic results with minimal sensitivity.
  4. Root Canal Therapy: Laser technology disinfects root canals more thoroughly than traditional methods, reducing bacteria and improving success rates.
  5. Crown Lengthening: Laser procedures reshape gum tissue to expose more of the tooth structure, facilitating restorative procedures like crowns or bridges.
  6. Treatment of Cold Sores and Aphthous Ulcers: Lasers can reduce pain, speed healing, and minimize recurrence by targeting the affected tissues directly.
  7. Frenectomy: Lasers remove or modify frenula (tongue or lip ties) with minimal discomfort, often avoiding sutures.
  8. Implantology: Lasers assist in soft tissue management around implants, improving healing and tissue integration.
  9. Tissue Management during Restorative Procedures: Lasers help in contouring and shaping soft tissues for better restorative outcomes.

Post procedure care:

Patients should follow their dentist's instructions carefully, which typically include maintaining good oral hygiene by gentle brushing and rinsing with a prescribed antimicrobial mouthwash to reduce bacteria. Avoiding hot, spicy, or abrasive foods helps prevent irritation, while sticking to soft, cool foods minimizes discomfort. It's important to abstain from smoking and alcohol, as they can impede healing. Pain or swelling may occur initially, and over-the-counter medications like ibuprofen can help manage discomfort. Regular follow-up appointments allow the Best Dentist in narasaraopet, Dr. Dodda Venkatesh Kumar to monitor healing progress. If any unusual symptoms such as excessive bleeding, severe pain, or signs of infection arise, prompt dental consultation is necessary. Adequate rest, hydration, and avoiding strenuous activity also support recovery. With attentive aftercare, patients can enjoy a smooth healing process and the long-term benefits of laser dental procedures.
